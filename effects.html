Effects on Ecosystems

Invasive species have a number of negative effects on the areas that have been invaded. They are a significant threat to the biodiversity by dominating an ecosystem, which can result in irreversible changes and widespread loss to the habitat.

Many of these species reproduce very effectively and their seeds can remain viable in the soil for years. They can also form hybrid species between the native and invasive plants which may cause displacement of the native species or even complete extinction.

Not only do they choke out native plants, invasive plants can also alter the water, nutrient, and energy cycles of the ecosystem, forever changing how the ecosystem functions.
